# Mearra Tone of Voice — Slide Copy Cheat Sheet

This is the short, practical voice guide. For the fuller positioning and brand story, see `brand-bible.md §1–2`.

Read this **before** writing any headline or body text. Copy that isn't in Mearra voice breaks the brand faster than a wrong colour does.

---

## The six-word signature

Mearra sounds **confident, optimistic, devoted, humane, quirky, active**.

If a line on your slide isn't at least three of those, rewrite it.

---

## Five rules, every headline

1. **Easy to understand.** Plain words. If it needs a glossary, it needs a rewrite.
2. **Straight-forward.** No poetry for the sake of poetry. Say it.
3. **Yes more than no.** Frame it positively unless the "no" is the point.
4. **Not neutral.** Lose the "ifs, buts, and maybes." Take a position.
5. **Proud, not cocky.** We're proud of the craft — for us *and* for our clients.

---

## Headline patterns that work

Use these as starting scaffolds. Each one is pulled from or modelled on approved brand copy.

### Short declaratives
- "Complexity gets us going."
- "We got you."
- "Small talk, all action."
- "Ready to get our hands dirty."
- "Creating outcomes worth keeping."

### Question–answer
- "You say difficult, we say 'where?'"
- "Where's the hard part? That's where we start."

### "Converting X to Y" / transformation
- "Converting complex to clarity."
- "Turning sales meetings into media plans."
- "Making media better, across the board."

### "Doing A, for B" / craft-for-outcome
- "Passioned about the tech, focused on your business."
- "Playing tech as our instrument."
- "Building outcomes that matter."

### "Between / beyond / beneath"
- "Between the layers of data, there's always a human."
- "Beyond the brief, into the business."

### The confident imperative
- "Let the sales meeting do the work."
- "Stop rebuilding. Start evolving."

## Body copy moves

- **Short sentences.** Period. Then the next one.
- **Active voice.** "We built," "we ship," "we cut." Not "was built by us."
- **Concrete nouns.** Not "solutions" — "a live-listening assistant." Not "platforms" — "Drupal," "MCP," "the planner."
- **Specific numbers.** 15 years, not "many years." 35 layouts, not "lots of layouts."
- **One idea per slide.** If you need "and also," you need another slide.

---

## Words & phrases to use

- *Hard stuff, tackle, solve, dismantle, unpack, untangle*
- *Outcome, impact, layer by layer, worth keeping*
- *Evolve, adapt, flow, in motion*
- *Human, hands-on, partner, together*
- *Bar-raising, ambitious, grit, depth*

## Words & phrases to avoid

- *Solutions, synergies, leverage, paradigm, seamless, holistic, end-to-end*
- *"We are pleased to…" / "We would be honoured to…"* (too formal, hedgy)
- *Maybe, perhaps, could potentially, might eventually* — no hedging.
- *Revolutionary, game-changing, world-class* — cocky, undifferentiated.
- *Industry-leading* — show it, don't say it.

---

## Capitalisation & punctuation

- **Headings:** Sentence case by default. Title Case only when it reads like a Title (product names, section dividers).
- **Slogan:** Always "Business. People. Tech. Together." Four capitalised words, three periods, one full stop.
- **Em dashes** are fine and Mearra-feeling — they add pace and make asides land. Don't overdo them.
- **Exclamation marks:** "Thank you!" at the close is fine. Elsewhere, rarely. The voice is confident, not loud.

---

## Audience tuning

Mearra voice stays Mearra across audiences. But the dial moves.

| Audience | Dial | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Client pitch | Sharper claims, more craft | "We'll ship the live-listening assistant in 4 weeks." |
| Internal all-hands | Warmer, more quirky | "We've shipped a thing. It's quietly excellent." |
| Board / steering | More structured, slightly more formal | "Quarter in review: three wins, two open risks." |
| Product / marketing | Punchier, more playful | "Small talk, all action." |
| Steering group | Facts + decisions needed | "Decision required: continue Phase 2 as scoped (yes / no)." |

---

## Red flags when writing slide copy

If any of these appear in a draft, rewrite before shipping:

- More than one qualifier per sentence ("typically, often, may potentially…").
- Passive voice as the main clause.
- A headline longer than the body text line below it.
- A brag without a receipt.
- A metaphor that doesn't earn its slide.
- Any line that *could* have been written by any consultancy. Mearra lines should sound like Mearra.

---

## One-paragraph gut check

After writing a deck, read the cover and closing slides aloud. If the deck were stripped of logos and colors, would you still know it was Mearra? If not, the voice hasn't landed. Go back, cut three adjectives, and pick one better verb.
