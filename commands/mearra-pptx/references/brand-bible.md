# Mearra Brand Bible

This is the authoritative reference for any Mearra-facing output. When building a deck, every color, typeface, headline, illustration, and photo must be justifiable against this document. If something isn't in here, it doesn't belong in a Mearra deck.

**Source:** `Mearra_BrandIdentity_2026_LA.pdf` (39 pages, 14 April 2026).

---

## 1. Essence & positioning

### Founding story
Mearra was founded in 2010 by business and open-technology experts in Northern Europe. The purpose from day one: genuinely partner with clients through deep passion for technology.

> "Our name embodies the energy of the sea: propelling digital evolution, navigating with agility, and accelerating the flow between complex systems and users."

Instead of just *navigating* the digital landscape, Mearra **clears the path** — providing capability and momentum for partners to evolve with confidence.

### Who we are
> "From 2010, Mearra has grown from a Drupal specialist into a business-driven, ambitious, and highly skilled team of experts ready to get their hands dirty with technology for the benefit of large organisations and their businesses. With us, there's no need to dread what's to come, because solving the hard stuff is what gets us going."

### Why we exist
> "To solve the business-critical and complex challenges our clients face and act as their partner in the ever-evolving world of technology. We build outcomes, layer by layer, that matter even when conditions change. At the same time, we want to raise the bar in the industry we operate in."

### The three pillars
1. **Focused on business** — Business is our North Star.
2. **Led by humans** — Humans working for humans; we never forget the human between the layers of data.
3. **Built with technology** — Technology is the tool of our craft.

### Slogan
**"Business. People. Tech. Together."**

The slogan always appears as four words, each capitalised, separated by periods. It belongs on cover slides and closing slides; it appears in the lower-left of every content slide as a small tagline (the template handles this automatically).

### Main messages
- **Business-critical problem solver:** "We tackle the business-critical, hard stuff with tech as our tool."
- **Evolution through collaboration:** "To evolve with our clients, and because of them, is what we strive for."
- **Excellence through ambition:** "Comfort rarely creates anything worth keeping, but ambition does."

### Elevator pitch
> "We exist to dismantle complexity, working toward outcomes that matter even when conditions change. We're here for the challenges that require grit, technical depth, and an understanding of both business and humans. When you say it's difficult, we say: 'Where?'"

## 2. Tone of voice

See also `tone-of-voice.md` for a slide-copy-focused cheat sheet.

### Six voice characteristics
Mearra is **confident, optimistic, devoted, humane, quirky, active**.

1. **Confident.** We can be trusted because we know what to do. You can hear the assertiveness.
2. **Optimistic.** There's always a silver lining. Can-do attitude, positive outlook.
3. **Devoted.** Passionate and proud of the work; don't hesitate to show it.
4. **Humane.** There are humans behind the brand. Word choices are firstly approachable.
5. **Quirky.** We dare to be ourselves. Not to annoy anyone, but to express ourselves.
6. **Active.** Who else is saying it if we don't? Yes, it was us who did it.

### Five execution rules
1. **Make it easy to understand.** Use words easy for your audience. Avoid industry jargon. If there's no easier way, ask whether you should say it at all.
2. **Be straight-forward.** Say it how it is. Avoid phrases that are too poetic or metaphorical. Prefer shortness and clear structure.
3. **Use yes more than no.** Same thing, positive outlook. Use "no" deliberately.
4. **Have courage to not be neutral.** Dare to have an opinion. Lose the "ifs, buts and maybes." Irony and humour OK when wise and understandable.
5. **Be proud, never cocky.** Proud of the work — for us, for our clients, and for the industry.

### Verbal personality
- **Register:** Conversational yet professional. Approachable without being casual.
- **Sentences:** Short. Clear structure. Avoid complexity for its own sake.
- **Voice:** Active ("we do," "we solve," "we tackle"). No hedging.
- **Headlines:** Imperative or declarative. Confidence first. Questions are welcome when they provoke.

## 3. Logo system

### Philosophy
> "The concept of the visual identity comes from the name Mearra — the sea and the droplets."

Two forms exist:
1. **Primary logo** (wordmark + symbol) — use whenever possible.
2. **Symbol** (mark only) — for tight spaces or when brand is already clearly identifiable (e.g., a content slide where the cover already carried the full mark).

### Placement rules
- **Clearspace:** Equal to the length of the symbol's "leg" (unit = x). Maintain at least `x` on all sides.
- **Minimum size:** Primary logo 25 mm print / 100 px digital; Symbol 8 mm print / 25 px digital.
- In Mearra decks:
  - Cover slides: primary logo, usually lower-left or centered depending on layout.
  - Content slides: symbol only, bottom-right.
  - Closing slides: primary logo or wordmark.

### Do nots (strict)
- Don't change the logo typography.
- Don't use non-brand colors.
- Don't apply effects (shadow, glow, blur).
- Don't use the wordmark alone — the symbol must be present.
- Don't stretch, rotate, recolor outside palette, or modify.
- Don't use raster sources when a vector is available.

## 4. Color palette — the only colors allowed

### Warm (primary visual signature)
| Name | HEX | RGB | CMYK |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Blossom** (pink) | `#ffa5d2` | 255 · 165 · 210 | 0/47/0/0 |
| **Coral** (red)   | `#ff5c5f` | 255 · 92 · 95   | 0/76/52/0 |

### Cool
| Name | HEX | RGB | CMYK |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Indigo** (purple) | `#4400f0` | 68 · 0 · 240   | 89/77/0/0 |
| **Trench** (navy)   | `#1e0050` | 30 · 0 · 80    | 100/100/34/48 |
| **Lagoon** (teal)   | `#00bebe` | 0 · 190 · 190  | 71/0/32/0 |
| **Current** (green) | `#00f087` | 0 · 240 · 135  | 61/0/70/0 |

### Neutrals
| Name | HEX | RGB |
|---|---|---|
| **Mist**  | `#ebe6e0` | 235 · 230 · 224 |
| **White** | `#ffffff` | 255 · 255 · 255 |
| **Black** | `#000000` | 0 · 0 · 0 |

### Philosophy
> "The Mearra colour palette ranges from warm to cooler tones, with the warmer colours forming the most distinctive part of the brand expression."

Rule of thumb for a deck: roughly 80% neutral backgrounds (Mist, White, Indigo, Trench), 15% warm accents (Blossom, Coral), 5% cool highlights (Lagoon, Current). Warm carries the signature; cool carries the depth; neutrals carry the room.

### Tints & gradients
- 100% opacity is the default. Tints permitted at **5 / 25 / 50 / 75 / 95%** only.
- Gradients use at most **3 colours**. White, Black, and Mist are supportive; they're fine as the neutral anchor in a gradient but don't count as the "statement" colour.

**Approved gradients** (use these, not ad-hoc mixes):
- Blossom → Coral
- Indigo → Blossom
- Blossom → Trench
- Indigo → Trench
- Indigo → Lagoon
- Lagoon → Trench
- Blossom → Coral → Current
- Lagoon variations at varying opacity
- Trench variations at varying opacity

### Accessibility
Every text-over-colour combo is tested against WCAG AA (<18 pt) and AA18 (≥18 pt). Unsuitable combinations may appear in illustrations to preserve richness, but **never** behind legible text.

Safe pairings to lean on:
- Trench text on Mist background — primary heading pattern.
- Mist or White text on Indigo or Trench — primary cover pattern.
- Trench text on Blossom — warm accent card (watch contrast at small sizes).
- White text on Coral — short punchy callouts only.

## 5. Typography

### Families
- **Sofia Sans** — body (Regular, Medium).
- **Sofia Sans Extra Condensed** — headings (Bold, ExtraBold, Black; often italic).

Both from Google Fonts.

> "Sofia Sans is a versatile neo-grotesque sans-serif typeface that balances clarity and functionality with subtle warmth. Its low stroke contrast and softly rounded terminals create a contemporary yet approachable tone."

The geometric softness of Sofia Sans echoes the rounded corners of the Mearra symbol — logo and type read as one system.

### Hierarchy
- **Headings** → Sofia Sans Extra Condensed, Bold/ExtraBold/Black. Italic by default; switch to upright when the headline is long or dense enough that italic hurts readability.
- **Body** → Sofia Sans, Regular or Medium. Use Medium for call-outs and lead-ins; Regular for long-form.

### Practical rules
- Never substitute a similar font. Always Sofia Sans / Sofia Sans Extra Condensed.
- Pair big italic condensed headings with warm accents (Blossom, Coral) for maximum brand signature.
- Avoid all-caps as a stylistic default; the condensed italic does the visual work.
- Line length ≤ 80 characters in body text.

## 6. Graphic elements — Aquatic Forms

### Concept
> "The visual expression reflects movement, the rounded shapes are inspired by the influence of water. Collaboration plays a key role in the identity, which is reflected in its graphic elements: connection & working together."

Keywords: **warm, collaboration, peace of mind, sea, in motion.**

### The illustration system
> "Mearra's illustration style is soft and playful, providing a contrast to the often angular visual language of the technology industry. The illustrations are built by combining modular shapes of varying forms, sizes, and colours. These elements, Aquatic forms, come together like visual building blocks, creating flexible compositions that can expand, adapt, and evolve across different contexts."

### Construction
- Developed as **black & white base forms**.
- **Modular**: combine, scale, arrange freely.
- **Color** is applied via **gradient maps** — this is how the forms pick up the palette without ever being "painted" directly.
- **Grain texture** at **5–25%** depending on scale. Grain must be consistent across a composition.

### Usage
- **Tight crops are the default.** Let shapes extend beyond the frame. Top-left, bottom-right, right edge bleeding off — all good.
- Cropping creates a sense of scale and immersion, "allowing shapes to extend beyond the frame and feel larger than the composition itself."
- Don't treat Aquatic Forms as literal representations of things. They're form, light, and texture — not diagrams.

The skill ships with 12 pre-rendered Aquatic Forms in `assets/aquatic-forms/` (blobs, clusters, circles, squares — all in brand-approved gradients). Use `deck.add_aquatic_form(name, slide, ...)` to drop them in.

## 7. Imagery

### Photography
> "The photography style brings personality through movement, light, and atmosphere, using visual elements that echo the rest of the identity. Images may be photographed, generated, or adapted from stock photography, as long as they follow the same visual direction. Stock images can often be modified through lighting, colour treatment, and cropping to align with the Mearra visual language."

- **Sources allowed:** original photography, AI-generated imagery, modified stock.
- **Modifications:** lighting, color treatment, tight crops.
- **Feel:** dynamic movement, strong light, atmosphere, personality.

5 atmospheric photos ship in `assets/backgrounds/`. Use them as full-bleed hero images (preferably with a soft dark overlay for text contrast).

### Icons
Ship with the skill as a line-icon set of 47 named icons (see `../ASSETS.md`). Use them sparingly — one per concept. Don't stack multiple icon styles.

## 8. Layout principles

The PDF doesn't define a numeric grid, but the principles are clear:

- **Whitespace is a value.** "Peace of mind" is a brand keyword. Resist the urge to fill.
- **Rounded over angular.** No hard 90° wedges. Let Aquatic Forms soften the frame.
- **Asymmetry is welcome.** Shapes extending beyond the frame naturally create asymmetrical, dynamic layouts.
- **Logo lives in predictable places.** Template handles this — don't move it.

## 9. Presentation do's and don'ts

### Do
- Use Blossom and Coral as the dominant visual accents.
- Pair big italic condensed headings with an Aquatic Form or an atmospheric photo.
- Apply tight cropping to both photos and illustrations.
- Build visual interest through movement and light, not through dense information.
- Use gradients (max 3 colors) for subtle depth on section headers or cover slides.
- Keep the slogan on cover and closing slides; let the template handle in-slide tagging.

### Don't
- Don't use hard-edged, angular shapes (contradicts the water metaphor).
- Don't overcomplicate layouts with too many elements.
- Don't use any colour outside the palette.
- Don't lose the human element — balance tech coolness with warmth.
- Don't compromise accessibility for style.

## 10. Anti-patterns — the hard "no" list

### Logo
- Don't change the logo typography.
- Don't use non-brand colours.
- Don't apply effects.
- Don't use the wordmark alone without the symbol.
- Don't alter proportions, rotate, or modify.
- Don't use raster sources where a vector is available.

### Voice
- No poetic or metaphorical filler.
- No neutrality — no "ifs, buts, and maybes."
- No unexplained jargon.
- No cockiness. Proud, not arrogant.
- Sarcasm only when wise and understandable.

### Visual
- No hard angles or sharp shapes.
- No effects on logos or illustrations.
- No recoloring outside the palette.
- No colour pairings that fail accessibility where text lives.
- No gradients with more than 3 colours.
- No losing the grain (5–25%).
- No literal-narrative illustrations — form, light, and texture win over representation.

### Application
- Don't use the monochrome version unless space constraints demand it.
- Don't place the logo on busy backgrounds without clearspace.
- Don't substitute for Sofia Sans.
- Don't fight whitespace. Embrace it.
- Don't create chaotic density — it breaks "peace of mind."
